# Fun But Harmful Viruses

These are “viruses” that act as funny pranks on a computer. They are meant to surprise or confuse the user for a moment, but they **can still be harmful**, annoying, or disruptive if used irresponsibly.

These types of programs usually do things like open random windows, move the mouse, spam messages, or create strange effects on the screen. They don’t exist to steal data or destroy systems — they exist mostly as jokes or demonstrations.

# Why?

Play pranks on your friends — literally.

Imagine someone using their computer normally and suddenly random pop-ups start appearing, the mouse begins moving on its own, or the screen starts doing weird things. For a moment they have **no idea what’s happening**, which makes it surprising (and sometimes funny).

These prank programs are often designed to be confusing, unexpected, and a little impressive from a technical perspective.

# What is my goal?

My goal is to bring back the idea of **harmless, funny, and creative computer prank programs** — the kind people used to make just for fun or to demonstrate programming skills.

Years ago, programmers would create small joke programs that made strange visual effects, funny pop-ups, or unexpected behavior on the screen. They were more about **creativity and curiosity** than about damaging computers.

I want to recreate that feeling: programs that are interesting, surprising, and a little impressive — while still being clearly meant as jokes.

Of course, these should always be used responsibly and only in situations where everyone involved is okay with the prank.
